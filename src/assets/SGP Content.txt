Types of Companies:
Heading: The Kind of Businesses We Back
Subtext: We look for software companies with strong fundamentals, durable advantages, and
clear room to grow.
● Markets with room to grow: Not niche ceilings but markets with real, expanding
demand.
● Defensibility that compounds: Advantages that deepen as the business scales, not
disappear.
● Diversified customer base: No single point of dependency, just consistent, distributed
revenue.
● Products customers don’t replace: Deeply embedded, high-value software that’s hard
to switch away from.

Stage &amp; Growth Context:
Heading: No hard filters. Just honest criteria.
Subtext: We typically invest in companies that look something like this:

● Revenue: $2M–$30M, with more than half recurring
● Growth: 10%+ annually (healthy, sustainable, not hockey-stick dependent)
● EBITDA: $500K+ (we back profitable businesses, not burning platforms)
● Churn: Under 10% (because sticky customers are the only ones that matter)
● Market: Large, growing, fragmented, with room to win

Where we add value:
Heading: Where We’re Most Useful
Subtext: We partner at the moments where growth becomes less obvious and more important
to get right.
● The inflection point ($5M–$20M ARR): You’ve outgrown founder-led sales, but haven’t
yet built the systems to scale.
● The “what’s next” moment: The business is profitable and working but the path
forward requires capital and clarity, without unnecessary dilution
● The M&amp;A acceleration phase: Growth by acquisition starts to make more sense than
organic alone, but you don’t yet have the infrastructure to source, execute, and integrate
deals.
● The talent ceiling: The next stage requires senior leadership: CRO, Head of Product,
FP&amp;A and getting those hires right changes everything.

The challenge: You built the business on founder relationships and referrals. Now you need a

What we do:
● Define ICP and segment the market
● Build repeatable pipeline and outbound motion
● Hire and support CRO / sales leadership
● Improve pricing, packaging, and expansion
● Turn CS into a growth function
2. Product &amp; AI
The challenge: Your product works but the roadmap is reactive, engineering is stretched, and
AI is everywhere without clear direction.
What we do:
● Prioritize roadmap with structured audits
● Bring in experienced product operators
● Focus AI where it actually matters
● Support engineering leadership and structure
● Guide build vs. buy vs. partner decisions
3. Talent &amp; Org Design

The challenge: The team that got you here won’t necessarily get you to the next stage.
Knowing what to change and when is one of the hardest parts of scaling.
What we do:
● Assess leadership, honestly
● Hire key roles: CRO, CFO, CTO, VP Product
● Evolve org structure as you scale
● Benchmark compensation
● Build performance without losing culture
4. M&amp;A &amp; Acquisitions
The challenge: You see opportunities for acquisition, but lack the infrastructure to source,
execute, and integrate deals effectively.
What we do:
● Identify high-fit acquisition targets
● Structure and finance deals
● Lead integration—operational + cultural
● Build platform + add-on strategy early

The 6 Value Propositions SGP's New Site Must Own:

VALUE POINT 1: Operators First, Investors Always — Not the Other Way
Around
The insight: Every PE firm claims to be "hands-on." Founders are tired of the word. What they
actually want is someone who has sat in their seat — who has debugged product, negotiated
enterprise deals, hired a VP Sales, and rebuilt a churn-leaking CS function. SGP's founder
Sumit Garg built companies before he funded them. That's the story.
How to say it:
"We've been in your seat. Not metaphorically — actually. Sumit Garg built software
companies before he invested in them. That difference isn't a footnote. It's
everything."

VALUE POINT 2: The Lower Middle Market Sweet Spot — Where Real
Builders Live
The insight: $2M–$30M ARR software companies are chronically underserved. Too small for
the Serents and Mainsails who are now writing $50M+ checks. Too proven for venture. SGP sits
in this exact gap — and it's a massive, fragmented market full of profitable, founder-owned
companies who need strategic capital, not just a wire transfer.
How to say it:
"You built something real. Profitable. Sticky. Growing. You're not a moonshot — and
you don't need to be. Most PE firms either want you bigger or smaller. We want you
exactly where you are, and we know exactly how to take you further."

VALUE POINT 3: The 48-Hour Promise — Respect for Founders' Time
The insight: Investment processes are notorious for destroying management focus. Founders
dread them. SGP's process — 48-hour go/no-go, LOI in 4–6 weeks, close in 16–24 weeks — is
legitimately faster and cleaner than most. This deserves to be a headline, not a footnote in a
PDF.
How to say it:

"We give you a yes or no in 48 hours. Not because we're reckless — because
we've done this long enough to know quickly. Your company doesn't pause while
we decide. Neither should you."

VALUE POINT 4: Legacy Over Exits — Long-Term Investing Without Fund
Timeline Pressure
The insight: Most PE firms are beholden to 5–7 year fund cycles. This creates misalignment —
they need an exit whether the company is ready or not. SGP's long-term capital model means
they're not engineering a sale. They're building a company. Founders who want to build
something lasting — not just get bought — need to hear this.
How to say it:
"We don't invest to exit. We invest to build. No fund clock pressure. No forced sale
when you're just hitting your stride. We're with you until the outcome is actually right
— for the company, not just the calendar."

VALUE POINT 5: M&amp;A as a Growth Lever, Not an Afterthought
The insight: SGP has quietly built a bolt-on M&amp;A track record (Extol to Cleo, CloudMonix +
Stackify to Netreo, Xpandion to Appsian, ClientSavvy to ClearlyRated). For founders thinking
about being acquired or becoming acquirers themselves, this is a genuine value-add that few
LMM firms articulate well.
How to say it:
"Growth doesn't only come from the inside. We've built platform + add-on strategies
for our portfolio companies — sourcing, financing, and integrating acquisitions that
compound value and accelerate market position."

VALUE POINT 6: Software-Only Focus — Depth Over Breadth
The insight: Generalist PE firms that "also do software" cannot match the pattern recognition,
operational expertise, and network of a firm that has only ever done software. SGP's exclusive
B2B software focus means every playbook, every relationship, every insight has been earned in
this exact context.
How to say it:

"We've never invested in a restaurant, a distribution company, or a roll-up. Only
software. That focus isn't a limitation — it's why we see things other investors miss,
and why founders trust us to get it right."
